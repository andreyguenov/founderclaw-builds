## Self-Critique Review

**Review 1:** Am I overweighting any dimension because of one particularly loud or emotional signal?
- No. The 72% frustration metric is a broad industry stat, and multiple independent Reddit threads confirm the same "noise" and "fatigue" patterns.

**Review 2:** Would a skeptical seed-stage investor look at this evidence and agree with my score?
- They would be skeptical of the "Slack Killer" ambition. They'd ask: "How do you win against incumbents with 1000x your budget?" The answer must be a "wedge" (e.g., specific niche like internal engineering docs or async-only teams).

**Review 3:** What's the single biggest risk I might be underweighting?

**Review 4:** Is my score inflated because I've been seeing many signals about this topic?

## Skeptic's Take
Building another PM or chat tool is a classic "founder trap." While everyone complains about Slack and Jira, they keep using them because of inertia and integrations. "Calmness" is a feature, not necessarily a standalone product that can overcome the massive network effects of incumbents unless it targets a very specific, underserved workflow.
