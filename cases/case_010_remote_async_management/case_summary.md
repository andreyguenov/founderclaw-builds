## Problem Statement
Remote companies promise async flexibility but managers lack tools to manage async teams confidently, causing them to revert to synchronous "presence checks" and loyalty tests that break the async model.

## Score Breakdown

### 1. Problem Severity: 7/10 (×2 = 14/20)
**Justification:** Broken promises ("async flexibility" → forced 7am presence checks). "Hate it" emotional signal. Manager framing it as "loyalty" = trust erosion. Affects work-life balance and autonomy. Real pain but need more evidence of scale.

### 2. Market Signal Strength: 3/10 (×2 = 6/20)
**Justification:** 1 signal, very recent (1 week ago). High specificity but no engagement data, no cross-source validation. Single complaint. Weak signal despite recency and emotional intensity.

### 3. Solution Gap: 7/10 (×2 = 14/20)
**Justification:** Async management tools exist (Twist, Notion, async check-ins) but clearly not solving the visibility problem that makes managers revert to presence checks. Gap is "manager confidence in async team output" not just async communication tools. High gap in growing async-first category.

### 4. Monetization Clarity: 7/10 (×1.5 = 10.5/15)
**Justification:** Remote-first companies (GitLab model, Basecamp, Zapier, Doist) are growing segment. Managers = decision-makers with budget. "Async" is trendy → companies want to be async-friendly → will pay for tools that enable it without surveillance. Clear buyer persona. Medium-high monetization clarity.

### 5. Build Feasibility: 6/10 (×1.5 = 9/15)
**Justification:** Async status tracking, output visibility, team health metrics. Integrates with existing tools (Slack, GitHub, Notion, Jira). 6-8 week MVP. Known patterns (status bots, digest reports). Medium feasibility.

### 6. Channel Accessibility: 5/10 (×1 = 5/10)
**Justification:** r/remotework accessible. Remote-first company Slack communities, async-first thought leaders (GitLab, Doist, Basecamp blogs). But niche market, hard to reach decision-makers (managers, not ICs). Medium channel access.

## Self-Critique

**Review 1:** Overweighting?
- Single signal but problem feels real because remote/async is a known trend. May be projecting category knowledge onto one complaint. Need more evidence.

**Review 2:** Would skeptical investor agree?
- **Would push back:** "One Reddit post is not a market. Remote work is mainstream now - if this were a real problem, someone would have built it. Show me a pattern." Valid concern.

**Review 3:** Biggest risk underweighted?
- **Behavior change problem.** Managers revert to presence checks because they don't trust output visibility, but also because they lack management skills. A tool won't fix a culture/skills gap. Risk is building a tool no one adopts because the root cause is human behavior, not lack of technology.

**Review 4:** Score inflation?
- Possibly. The problem resonates personally (async work is trending topic) but 1 signal from 1 week ago is not evidence of market demand. Keeping score but flagging as speculative.

**Adjustment:** No score changes but flagging as HIGH RISK for behavior change resistance.
