## Self-Critique Review

**Review 1: Am I overweighting any dimension because of one particularly loud or emotional signal?**  
No. The signal is secondhand and lacks emotion. I'm actually underweighting the potential severity because the evidence is so thin. If anything, I'm being too generous with the Monetization Clarity score (7/10) based on assumptions about Hollywood budgets rather than explicit evidence of willingness to pay for coordination software.

**Review 2: Would a skeptical seed-stage investor agree with my score?**  
Yes. An investor would see this as too early-stage. "Coordinators are frustrated" is not enough. What specific workflow? What did they try? How many coordinators feel this way? What's the TAM? This would get a "come back when you have customer interviews" response. Score of 45.5/100 is appropriate.

**Review 3: What's the single biggest risk I might be underweighting?**  
**Domain complexity risk.** Film production has decades of established workflows, union rules, industry-specific terminology, and regulatory requirements (e.g., SAG-AFTRA rules, location permits, insurance). Building a tool that Hollywood professionals would actually trust and adopt requires deep domain expertise. This is not a "learn as you build" situation. The risk that even a well-built MVP would be rejected for missing critical industry nuances is very high.

**Adjustment:** Build Feasibility should be lower. Changing from 4/10 to 3/10.

**Review 4: Is my score inflated because I've been seeing many signals about this topic?**  
No. This is the first and only signal about Hollywood/film production coordination. No inflation risk.
